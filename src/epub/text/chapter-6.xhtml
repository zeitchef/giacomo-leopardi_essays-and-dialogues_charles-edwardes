Dialogue Between Malambruno and Farfarello

Malambruno Spirits of the deep, Farfarello, Ciriatto, Raconero, Astarotte, Alichino, or whatever else you are called, I adjure you in the name of Beelzebub, and command you by virtue of my art, which can unhinge the moon, and nail the sun in the midst of the heavens, come one of you with your prince’s permission, to put all the powers of hell at my disposal.
Farfarello Here I am.
Malambruno Who are you?
Farfarello Farfarello, at thy service.
Malambruno Have you the mandate of Beelzebub?
Farfarello I have; and can thus do for thee all that the king himself could do, and more than lies in the power of all other creatures together.
Malambruno It is well. I wish to be satisfied in but one desire.
Farfarello Thou shalt be obeyed. What is it? Dost thou wish for majesty surpassing that of the Atrides?
Malambruno No.
Farfarello More wealth than shall be found in El Dorado, when it is discovered?
Malambruno No.
Farfarello An empire as large as that of which Charles V dreamt one night?
Malambruno No.
Farfarello A mistress chaster than Penelope?
Malambruno No: methinks the devil’s aid were superfluous for that.
Farfarello Honours and success, however wicked thou mayst be?
Malambruno I should rather more need the devil, if I wished the contrary, under such circumstances.
Farfarello Then what dost thou want?
Malambruno Make me happy for a moment.
Farfarello I cannot.
Malambruno Why?
Farfarello I give you my word of honour—I cannot do it.
Malambruno The word of honour of a good demon?
Farfarello Yes, to be sure. Thou shouldest know that there are good devils as well as good men.
Malambruno And you must know that I will hang you by the tail to one of these beams if you do not instantly obey me without more words.
Farfarello It were easier for you to kill me, than for me to satisfy your demands.
Malambruno Then return with my malediction, and let Beelzebub come himself.
Farfarello Beelzebub and the whole army of hell would be equally powerless to render you or any of your race happy.
Malambruno Not even for a single moment?
Farfarello As impossible for a moment, half a moment, or the thousandth part of a moment, as for a lifetime.
Malambruno Well, since you cannot make me happy in any way, at least free me from unhappiness.
Farfarello On condition that you no longer love yourself above everything else.
Malambruno I shall only cease doing that when I die.
Farfarello But as long as you live you will be unable to do it. Your nature would tolerate anything rather than that.
Malambruno So it is.
Farfarello Consequently, loving yourself above everything, you desire your own happiness more than anything. But because this is unattainable, you must necessarily be unhappy.
Malambruno Even when engaged in pleasure; since no gratification can make me happy, or satisfy me.
Farfarello Truly none.
Malambruno And because pleasure cannot satisfy my soul’s innate desire for happiness, it is not true pleasure, and during its continuance I shall still be unhappy.
Farfarello As you say: because in men and other living beings, the deprivation of happiness, even though pain and misfortune be wanting, implies express unhappiness. This, too, during the continuance of so-called pleasures.
Malambruno So that from birth to death our unhappiness never ceases for an instant.
Farfarello Yes, it ceases whenever you sleep dreamlessly, or when, from one cause or another, you are deprived of your senses.
Malambruno But never, so long as we are sensible that we live.
Farfarello Never.
Malambruno So that in fact it were better not to live than to live.
Farfarello If the absence of unhappiness be better than unhappiness itself.
Malambruno Then?
Farfarello Then if you would like to give me your soul before its time, I am ready to carry it away with me.
